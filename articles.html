The End of Seeing is Believing

Deepfakes, synthetic media, and the crumbling epistemic contract — what happens when AI dissolves the boundary between real and fabricated reality? For most of human history, visual evidence held a privileged epistemic status. Photographs were admissible in court. Video footage ended political careers. The camera was assumed to be an impartial witness. That assumption is now structurally broken. Generative models can produce photorealistic video of events that never occurred, voices indistinguishable from their originals, and documents that pass forensic scrutiny. The technical barrier to synthetic media has collapsed from nation-state capability to consumer hardware. What the blockchain offers — cryptographic provenance, immutable timestamping, decentralized attestation — may be the only viable infrastructure for rebuilding trust in digital media at scale. But provenance without adoption is just a technical curiosity.
